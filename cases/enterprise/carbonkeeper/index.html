Case Study: CarbonKeeper

Summary:

African CRM with automatic phone & SMS tracking

Needs:

When CarbonKeeper approached us, their key offering was a software product that manages customer records for carbon credit purposes.

To clarify, any home appliance such as a wood stove or solar lamp that reduces the carbon footprint of the area is valid for a carbon reduction credit on the international market.

So, many stoves and lanterns are sold at below-market prices, subsidized by the carbon credits that the distributor can sell on the global market. However, each distributor is audited, so it's important to record the yearly usage of each stove/lamp/etc.

Thus, in order to keep good records in case of an audit, the CarbonKeeper software was a great aid. However, the functionality was limited to web-based submission and SMS.

Our assignment was to experiment with Android-based CRM / data-entry app solutions for their audience, minimally-educated carbon product vendors in sub-saharan Africa.

Delivered:

We delivered a CRM app for Android, designed for rural merchants and optimized for two key use cases:

  1. Recording the initial sale
  2. Ongoing customer service

1. Recording the Sale

Key features:

  • Offline-capable forms

Recording the sale was facilitated with a simple offline-capable form, compatible with OpenDataKit.

2. Customer Service

Key features:

  • Custom call screen
  • Automatic Call-tracking
  • Automatic SMS-tracking
  • CRM Integration

Follow-up service calls were linked with the CarbonKeeper CRM database, so that all inbound calls from existing customers would trigger a custom call screen with their information on it.

After a customer phone call, the user is prompted to categorize and log any notes relevant to the call. All data is uploaded in the background to the CRM.

If the user doesn't fill out any data, a basic call log is still filed.

Result:

After showing the app to their customers and testing the waters, CarbonKeeper ended up deciding that this was a better opportunity than their original offering and changed their corporate direction to focus less on carbon products and more on general-purpose CRM & ERP for the developing world, rebranding as Keep Software.
