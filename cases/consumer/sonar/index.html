Case Study: Sonar.me

Summary:

Cutting-edge social app

Needs:

Sonar has come to us multiple times for assistance on their iPhone app.

In the early days, they needed to finish development of their iPhone app in time for SxSW. The code base was fairly complete, but had been written by a junior developer and had a few bugs in it. Our job was to come in and clean it out so that they would be able to launch in time for SxSW. Additionally, we were to instruct their developer in defensive programming techniques.

A year later, they called on us when they were re-launching the company at SxSW and were overhauling the app.

After the launch, they called us back in for additional help in developing new features.

Delivered:

First engagement:

We performed a line-by-line audit of the entire codebase, suggested fixes for each problem, and wrote up a set of bug-prevention techniques as to minimize bugs in the future.

Second engagement:

They had moved their Ruby developer to iOS, we we gave him some training to bring him up to speed.

Among other fixes, the biggest focus was moving from Foursquare-based location to proximity-based, venue-agnostic, location (running in the background). This way, we could show nearby people from any major social service: Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, FourSquare, or Sonar!

Third engagement:

The background location feature we introduced last time was a battery hog. We optimized the background location code.

A social app needs to grow! We implemented a slick social invitation feature that sent emails & SMSes to people of the addressbook.

What's more social than chat? Nothing! We built out our own real-time XMPP-based chat system, complete with status messages, just like Google Talk. It's one of our proudest accomplishments on mobile.

Result:

After our first engagement, they managed to get into the app store, bug free, for their launch at SxSW.

After our second engagement, they re-launched successfully at SxSW and saw some good press.

After our third engagement, they took runner-up at TechCrunch Disrupt, got some coverage and raised a healthy round of funding to continue their growth.

Our invite feature also invited 14,000+ users within the first week. Conversion rate: unknown. :|

Sonar is now in the deadpool, but not for our lack of trying!

Brett Martin

CEO, Sonar
