Case Study: QUADRO

Summary:

Drone AI w/ voice commands & face-recognition

Watch this on YouTube!Watch this on YouTube!

Needs:

After all the mobile apps we work with day-to-day,, we thought it might be interesting to experiment with some "defense" technology.

The concept: what if each soldier were issued a flying drone to aid him on the ground, almost like his personal pet.

That is, a drone that could scout around him and warn him of impending danger, without any cumbersome piloting involved.

Wouldn't that be neat?

Delivered:

In 24 hours, we wrote an autopilot for the Parrot AR Drone that would respond to voice commands and recognize faces.

In addition, the user can take manual control via a PlayStation controller in case any sort of precise commands are needed.

Result:

Unfortunately, QUADRO malfunctioned on stage and we were unable to fully demonstrate our technology.

The good news is that we got some great coverage from TechCrunch and inspired some members of the audience to attempt similar projects the following year.

We have some contacts at Boeing who are considering pursuing this technology in the future. Will they involve us? We hope so!
